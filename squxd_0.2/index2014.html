  • MY CLOSET | interaction designOpen or Close

    Interaction Design

    The Problem:
    Waking up in the morning not knowing what clean outfit to wear that will be comfortable for that day's weather.

    The Solution:
    MyCloset. A single application that manages your clothing and recommends outfits based on each day's weather forecast. This will not only save you time, but also keep you looking and feeling great throughout the day. It's like your personal stylist in the palm of your hand.

    How it Works:
    The home icon contains the weather, a scrolling list of recommendations, and your outfit for that particular day. Once you have uploaded photos of your clothing into the different categories within your Closet, easily navigate through to select something that may not be found in the recommended list. The Heart icon is a list of your favorite outfits that can be edited at any time. At the end of the day, discard your outfit into the Basket" and watch the percent in the lower left hand corner rise, thus indicating when you are getting close to needing to do laundry. After laundry day, simply click the top wash button on the Basket page to sort all of your dirty clothing back into their assigned category.

    MyCloset is Clean. Simple. Fun. What's yours?

  • HUMAN RIGHTS AWARENESS | installation designOpen or Close

    Installation Design

    Article 22
    Everyone, as a member of society, has the right to social security and is entitled to realization, through national effort and international co-operation and in accordance with the organization and resources of each State, of the economic, social and cultural rights indispensable for his dignity and the free development of his personality.


    Article 23
    Everyone has the right to work, to free choice of employment, to just and favorable conditions of work and to protection against unemployment. Everyone, without any discrimination, has the right to equal pay for equal work. Everyone who works has the right to just and favorable remuneration ensuring for himself and his family an existence worthy of human dignity, and supplemented, if necessary, by other means of social protection. Everyone has the right to form and to join trade unions for the protection of his interests.


    Article 24
    Everyone has the right to rest and leisure, including reasonable limitation of working hours and periodic holidays with pay.

    About the Project

    After being randomly assigned three Articles, it was our job as a group to determine an effective way of depicting the articles. My research led me to the inspirational photograph, Lunch atop a Skyscraper, and from there we created silhouettes of a teenage skateboader, middleage construction worker, and an elderly women. Each silhouette contains a collage of black and white photography that ranges from those who were unaware of their rights, to those more educated. We wanted these individual black and white silhouettes to pop off the background poster of each article, but be seen together as a whole.
